About

I spent the first eighteen years of my life on the island of Kwajalein, a US Army base in the Marshall Islands (about halfway between Hawaii and Australia). Kwajalein is roughly three miles long and one-half mile wide, with a population of around 1000 people. Due to its small size and remote nature, the island lacks many of the amenities of the mainland United States (including, most prominently, both cell phones and cars). Despite what others might view as a somewhat limited lifestyle, I owe who I am today to those eighteen years on that lovable rock.

However, all good things must come to an end, and so the end of my senior year of high school found me packing my bags in search of a new adventure at Stanford University, where I'm currently studying Computer Science and Physics. Every day, I'm struck by the incredible opportunities available to me as I explore Finance, Mathematics, French, Statistics, Game Theory, Cryptography, Socioeconomic Inequality, and so much more. I swing dance, sing, swim, bike, run, row, code, and above all, explore, and I love every minute of it.

My name is John Sholar, and this is my story so far.
